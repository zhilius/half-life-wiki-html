Multiplayer Overview

Half-Life's multiplayer component was a significant factor in its lasting success. The game shipped with a Deathmatch mode that supported up to 32 players. More importantly, Half-Life's modding community created some of the most influential multiplayer games in history.

Deathmatch

Classic arena-style multiplayer with all single-player weapons available. Maps ranged from small close-quarters arenas to large open spaces. Players competed for frags using the full Half-Life arsenal, including the iconic crowbar.

Team Fortress Classic

Included as part of the Half-Life: Game of the Year edition, Team Fortress Classic was a class-based team multiplayer game ported from the original Quake mod. It featured nine character classes including Scout, Soldier, Heavy, Medic, and Spy, with distinct abilities and weapons.

Modding Legacy

Half-Life's GoldSrc engine spawned one of the most vibrant modding communities in gaming history:

  • Counter-Strike (1999) — The most famous Half-Life mod, later becoming a standalone franchise and one of the most popular competitive FPS games ever.
  • Day of Defeat (2003) — World War II-themed team shooter.
  • Deathmatch Classic — A tribute to id Software's Quake deathmatch.
  • Natural Selection (2002) — A unique blend of FPS and real-time strategy.
  • Science and Industry — Team-based objective gameplay.
  • The Specialists — Matrix-style action movie mod.

The modding tools provided with Half-Life allowed anyone to create new maps, weapons, and game modes, leading to an explosion of creativity that defined online PC gaming in the early 2000s.

Map List

Notable multiplayer maps included Crossfire, Snark Pit, Bounce, Stalkyard, Subtransit, and Rats — the last being a miniature-scale map set in a kitchen.

Multiplayer Facts

  • Counter-Strike began as a Half-Life mod in 1999.
  • Valve hired the Counter-Strike development team.
  • Half-Life Deathmatch supported up to 32 players.
  • The modding SDK was released alongside the game.
