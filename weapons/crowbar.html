Overview

The Crowbar is the first weapon acquired in Half-Life and the most iconic weapon in the series. Found leaning against a wall in the Anomalous Materials Lab, it serves as Gordon Freeman's signature tool throughout the game.

Weapon Specifications

Crowbar Stats
Category Melee
Damage 10 per hit
Ammunition None
Range Very short (melee)
Rate of Fire Dependent on player
First Found Chapter 2: Anomalous Materials

Usage

The crowbar has unlimited use with no ammunition requirement. It can break wooden crates, open ventilation grates, and defeat enemies at close range. Its primary advantages are its infinite durability and silent operation — it never runs out and does not alert distant enemies.

Combat Effectiveness

  • Headcrabs and Zombies — Effective with careful timing
  • Military Personnel — Not recommended unless surprised
  • Xen Creatures — Situational, best used as a utility tool
  • Breakable Objects — Excellent for smashing crates and vents

Legacy

The crowbar has become one of the most recognizable weapons in video game history. It appears in every Half-Life game and has been referenced in countless other media. The crowbar is to Gordon Freeman what the lightsaber is to a Jedi — an instantly recognizable symbol of the franchise. Its simplicity and effectiveness embody the game's design philosophy: giving the player tools and letting them solve problems creatively.

Quick Facts

  • You can carry only one crowbar.
  • It never breaks or runs out.
  • It can break most wooden objects.
  • It deals no self-damage
