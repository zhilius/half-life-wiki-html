Expansion Overview

Half-Life received three official expansion packs, all developed by Gearbox Software under the supervision of Valve. Each expansion tells the story of the Black Mesa Incident from a different perspective, featuring new weapons, enemies, and areas of the facility.

Half-Life: Opposing Force (1999)

Opposing Force Details
ReleaseNovember 15, 1999
ProtagonistCorporal Adrian Shephard (HECU Marine)
DeveloperGearbox Software
Chapters21

Players take the role of Corporal Adrian Shephard, a HECU marine who becomes separated from his unit during the initial deployment. Shephard must survive both the alien invasion and a mysterious Black Ops team sent to eliminate all evidence — including the marines themselves. Opposing Force introduced new weapons (M249 SAW, shock roach, pipe wrench, spore launcher) and enemies (Race X aliens, Pit Drones, Shock Troopers, Gene Worm).

Half-Life: Blue Shift (2001)

Blue Shift Details
ReleaseJune 12, 2001
ProtagonistBarney Calhoun (Security Guard)
DeveloperGearbox Software
Chapters14

Players control Barney Calhoun, a security guard at Black Mesa. The expansion covers the same timeframe as the original Half-Life but from Barney's perspective. Blue Shift focused on the more human-scale experience of a regular employee, with fewer weapons and more emphasis on survival. It was also the first Half-Life game to feature high-definition model packs.

Half-Life: Decay (2001)

Decay Details
ReleaseNovember 14, 2001
ProtagonistsDr. Gina Cross & Dr. Colette Green
DeveloperGearbox Software
PlatformPlayStation 2 only (later ported to PC)
Chapters9 (co-operative campaign)

Decay is a co-operative expansion designed exclusively for the PlayStation 2 version of Half-Life. Two players control Dr. Gina Cross and Dr. Colette Green, scientists who must work together to contain the damage from the resonance cascade. The expansion features puzzles that require cooperation and a unique perspective on the opening moments of the disaster.

Expansion Timeline

All three expansions take place concurrently with the events of the original Half-Life. The stories intersect at various points — for example, Barney Calhoun briefly meets Gordon Freeman during the tram sequence, and Adrian Shephard's path crosses with Gordon's at several locations.

Expansion Facts

  • Opposing Force was the first game developed by Gearbox Software.
  • Blue Shift was originally developed as a bonus pack for the Dreamcast version.
  • Decay was exclusive to PS2 and required two players.
  • The expansions add a combined total of 44 new chapters.
